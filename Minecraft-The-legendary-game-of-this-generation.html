Minecraft: The legendary game of this generation

Minecraft is undoubtedly one of the greatest sandbox games of this generation. The game has spanned over a decade, gone through major ups and downs, and provided a platform for thousands of creators to experiment, play, and entertain. Minecraft is the best-selling video game of all time, with over 238 million copies sold and nearly 140 million monthly active players.

Minecraft allows gamers to dive deeply into a 3D universe where they’re in total control of every little detail. No you’re not hearing it wrong, you can literally build a universe from scratch thanks to Minecraft. The game is basically composed of rough objects like cubes and blocks, representing various materials like dirt, trees, water, and lava. Players will pick up these objects and use them as materials to build whatever they want. Legendary constructions like Minas Tirith of Lord of the Rings, Kings’ Landing in Games of Thrones, or even the whole Los Angeles City in real life, can all be recreated from the basic cubes and blocks that Minecraft offers.

Gamers all over the world are all too familiar with the competitive and storytelling game formats already, so Minecraft is definitely a breath of fresh air. The game is truly the symbol of creativity. It’s coded in the Javascript language, a very old-school coding language that modern programmers might look down on. Since every detail of the game is made of cubes and blocks, it doesn’t have the fancy look of today’s hottest games like Fortnite or GTA. This is exactly what the developers intended when they created Minecraft. They have a specific idea in mind that the cubes and blocks they provide are enough for everyone to stretch their imagination and bring whatever they have in mind into reality. The free-flow nature of the game appears to be a big risk at first, as many people are willing to bet on a game with a strong storyline rather than a simple game without a strong purpose. But there’s a philosophy that Minecraft wants to remind everyone. Human beings in this world started off with almost nothing, and then built up everything just by using raw materials that Mother Earth provides. Similarly in Minecraft, just by using some cubes and blocks, players can create any universe they have in mind. The game’s beauty lies in such simplicity that took the world time to understand, and when everyone did, Minecraft’s widely understood as an art form of the gaming industry.

Minecraft is now one of the most popular games in history. The game’s approach is very useful for players to acquire problem solving skills, architecture knowledge, and patience as a character. Many universities and educational institutions have even utilized Minecraft as a platform for students to further their learnings. This isn’t just a game, it’s a special legacy that we all can appreciate.
