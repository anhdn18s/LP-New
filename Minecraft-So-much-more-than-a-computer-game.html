Minecraft: So much more than a computer game

Since its release date in 2011, Minecraft has become a global sensation, captivating a generation of the world population. It’s now the best selling video games of all time, taking over the number one spot from Tetris and Wii Sports. The creator of Minecraft was giving the game for free for a few years, before establishing a game studio called Mojang that was sold to Microsoft for $2.5 billion.

There have been plenty of blockbuster games before, but Minecraft is a very unique phenomenon. The game itself isn’t quite a normal game. It’s more like a cultural destination, or a private space that gamers can invite people to join if they want to. Using cubes and blocks hidden in every material, players can literally build everything that they imagine. The game welcomes players to get under the hood, break things, fix stuff and make their visions come true. There’s no plots, no storyline like the usual gaming platforms that everyone’s accustomed to. Minecraft is the epitome of creativity, allowing the players to do whatever they want, with the unlimited resources that the game allocates.

Advertisement

Winning in video games is a very crucial factor in the game’s success, but in Minecraft you simply don’t win or lose. It presents plenty of exploration opportunities. You can build a Vatican church, the whole New York City, fight zombies, basically do anything in this game, but that’s not the point. The fun lies in the freedom of the game: making your own rules, creating your own universe or simply just hanging out with your friends. Minecraft isn’t the first sandbox game in history, but it’s the most special. The graphics of the game are very simple and basic, yet can serve as the building blocks of any massive universe. Together with its quaint visuals, its mix of exploration and creativity, its multiplayer functionality and the impeccable music, the game is truly a work of art.

The CEO Persson took the initiative to bring Minecraft as a tool to get children into programming, as the Mojang Studio created a Minecraft Hour of Code tutorial to teach fans of the game some basic elements of coding. They even established a Minecraft Education Edition to assist hundreds of schools around the world to teach everything through Minecraft: from Computer Science, Architecture to Theater Studies. Rather than being just a computer game, Minecraft has truly become a legend as a place where people can really build alternate universes and realities, while having all the freedom to express themselves.
